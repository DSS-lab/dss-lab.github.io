Prospective Graduates Students

I am always looking for bright and motivated students with CS/CE/EE/ECE backgrounds. Candidates are expected to have solid background/interest in one of the following areas:

• Computer Security and Embedded Systems

• Power Grid Cyber-Physical Systems

• Control Theory and Optimization

• Machine Learning and Deep Neural Networks

Required Skills/Criteria:

• Strongly motivated

• Excellent programming skills: C/C++, Python, Matlab

IMPORTANT: The deadline for graduate students for the Summer and Fall semesters is March 1st. The application deadline for the Spring semester is October 1st.

If you would like to work under my supervision, please contact me at konstantinou at caps.fsu.edu if interested. Attach your transcripts, resume, and any publications. In addition, send me the a short summary that:

1) Shows you have already looked at my previous work.

2) Tells me the research direction(s) you are interested in.

3) Mentions any relevant background you might have for these directions.

I will usually have a Skype call with promising candidates. To show me you have actually taken the time to read this page, include “Sunshine State” at the beginning of your email. Finally, I highly recommend to read first Prof. Wilson's guide for joining my lab.

Please accept my apology that due to the large number of emails I receive, only shortlisted candidates will be replied.

Prospective Undergraduate Students

I am always looking for undergraduates to assist in investigating how our critical infrastructures can be more secure and more resilient. In addition to conducting research, participants will work on professional development (scientific literature, science communication, professional networking), and will present their research findings at our weekly lab meetings. The positions are primarily based at the CAPS, FSU.

Interested applicants should email to Dr. Konstantinou with:

1) A resume/CV that includes relevant coursework and research experience.

2) A cover letter that describes interest and how the research experience fits with future goals and aspirations. Students from primarily undergraduate institutions and underrepresented groups are encouraged to apply.

NEWS: Spring and Summer 2020 We have Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REUs) positions available for the spring and summer of 2020 to work on projects related to hardware security and power systems.
